1. Non‑501(c)(3) Disclosure & Tax Treatment of Gifts

Universal Gospel Fellowship Center (“UGFC,” “the Ministry,” “we,” “us”) is intentionally NOT organized as a 501(c)(3) tax‑exempt entity under the United States Internal Revenue Code. This is a conscious decision rooted in our conviction that the assembly of believers (the ekklesia) ought to remain free of governmental conditions on what may be preached, taught, or declared from the pulpit and in the field.

Gift framing — the basis on which we receive support

All money received by UGFC — whether characterized on this site as a seed offering, a freewill gift, a ministry contribution, a tier offering, a monthly subscription offering, a lifetime offering, or simply a donation — is received as a gift to a ministry, not as a purchase of consumer goods or services. Participants who give toward a course, mentorship, ordination, or any other tier receive teaching materials, program access, and ministry care in response to that gift, at the Ministry’s sole discretion and according to the Ministry’s order — not as merchandise sold under the Uniform Commercial Code or under any consumer-protection statute governing purchased goods.

Grace, not contract

Every teaching, recording, mentorship interaction, ordination decision, and ministry response provided in response to a gift is delivered as a matter of the Ministry’s grace and discretion, not as a contractual deliverable. No participant has a contractual right to any specific quantum of teaching, specific session duration, specific minister contact, specific outcome, or specific schedule. The Ministry retains absolute discretion over the substance, pacing, format, and timing of all teachings and ministry care, and may modify, suspend, or end any program element when, in the Ministry’s sole judgment, that action serves the Kingdom purpose for which the Ministry exists. The gift is the act of sowing; the teaching is the Ministry’s discretionary response in stewardship of that gift before the Most High.

What this means for you, the supporter

  • Offerings, seed gifts, monthly subscription contributions, lifetime contributions, and freewill support given to UGFC are not tax‑deductible as charitable contributions on your federal or state income tax return.
  • UGFC does not issue IRS‑qualified donation receipts (such as a 501(c)(3) acknowledgment letter).
  • You are giving as a believer supporting a ministry, not as a taxpayer seeking a deduction.
  • You are giving as a supporter sowing into the work, not as a consumer purchasing a returnable product.

If charitable tax deduction is important to you, please direct gifts intended for that purpose to a 501(c)(3) organization of your choosing. We will not be offended.

6. Privacy Policy (TDPSA, CCPA/CPRA, COPPA)

Our voluntary commitment. UGFC may qualify as a small business under the United States Small Business Administration size standards, which would generally exempt the Ministry from most provisions of the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA, Texas Business and Commerce Code Chapter 541). We have chosen to honor the rights granted by the TDPSA and by the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA) anyway, because we believe that handling the personal information of believers with care is part of walking honorably before the Most High.

What we collect

  • Your name, email address, and (where applicable) mailing address — when you join the mailing list, submit a contact form, apply to a program, or complete an offering.
  • Payment card details — processed by our current payment processor (Stripe) directly; UGFC does not store full card numbers on its servers.
  • Application answers — for programs that require them.
  • Basic site analytics (page visits, referral source, device type) collected by Cloudflare. We do not use third-party advertising cookies and we do not engage in cross-site behavioral tracking.

Sensitive data — religious affiliation

Under TDPSA Section 541.001(23) and similar provisions of the CCPA/CPRA, information that reveals a person’s religious beliefs is classified as sensitive personal data. By the nature of this Ministry, your name and email on our mailing list reveal your interest in a Christian ministry, which may itself reveal your religious affiliation. We treat this data accordingly:

  • We do not sell your religious-affiliation data, your name, your email, or any other personal information, to anyone, for any consideration, monetary or otherwise.
  • We do not use your personal information to target advertising to you on third-party platforms.
  • We do not profile you for automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

How we use what we collect

To deliver the program or offering you signed up for; to communicate ministry updates, prayer requests, teaching content, and announcements; to process offerings; to respond to your inquiries; to maintain records required by tax and regulatory law; and to comply with our legal obligations.

Who we share it with

Our third-party processors only, each acting under their own published privacy terms and only to perform services for the Ministry:

  • Stripe — payment processing (our current payment processor; subject to change with notice posted to this page);
  • MailerLite — email delivery (where applicable; subject to change with notice posted to this page);
  • Formspree — form intake and submission delivery;
  • Cloudflare — hosting, DNS, and basic site analytics.

We do not sell your personal information to any party.

Regardless of whether you reside in Texas, California, or elsewhere, UGFC voluntarily grants the following rights to all visitors and supporters:

  • You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you;
  • You may request correction of inaccurate data;
