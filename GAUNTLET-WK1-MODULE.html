Lesson One

Scriptural Basis for Spiritual Warfare & Deliverance

Learning about spiritual warfare, what spiritual deliverance is, the Kingdoms involved in the war, and the spiritual authority Yasha—Christ gave His disciples throughout all ages within the context of self-deliverance is an integral part of, and a continuation of, Yasha’s ministry for His disciples of today — and is a core part of the gospel.

Matthew 12:29 · Matthew 15:21–28 · Mark 7:24–30 · Revelation 1:18

A · What Is Spiritual Deliverance?

Spiritual deliverance is for all disciples of Yasha—Christ who have believed the gospel and confessed with their own voice that Yasha—Christ is the Adon and Master over their lives. Deliverance encompasses body, soul, and spirit cleansing and inner healing. Deliverance from evil spirits, emotional and mental illnesses, along with physical illnesses and diseases, are easily obtained and maintained by believers who are committed to a lifestyle of holiness and service unto the Adon — while still living in a fallen, broken, dark world.

Isaiah 61:1 · Luke 4:14–21

B · The Two Opposing Kingdoms: AhaYah Our Creator’s vs. Satan Our Adversary’s

Discover, explore, and understand the operation of the Kingdom of Light — our AhaYah, our Creator’s legal realm — that brings faith, love, joy, peace, and hope, compared to the realm that our adversary Satan presents and tempts humans into embracing, causing untold suffering, shame, guilt, and condemnation.

The Kingdom of Light is our Creator’s Kingdom and the realm we should all want to live in for eternity:

Daniel 7:13–14 · Matthew 6:33 · Mark 1:14–15 · Luke 6:20 · Luke 13:18–21 · Luke 17:20–21 · John 8:1 · Colossians 1:13 · 1 John 1:5 · 1 Peter 2:9

Realize the stark difference of Satan’s illegal realm that has kept human beings in bondage from the beginning:

Luke 22:31–34 · John 3:19–20 · Ephesians 6:12 · Colossians 1:13 · 1 John 5:19

C · The Authority of the Disciple of Christ, Using His Name

Learn about the spiritual authority given to all believers by examining Yasha’s ministry in detail — and you will find your authority and identity are in Yasha—Christ and in His Name alone.

Psalm 8:4–6 · Matthew 18:18 · Matthew 28:18–20 (the Great Commission for ALL believers) · Mark 16:15–20 · Luke 10:18–24 · John 16:33 · John 17:26 · 2 Corinthians 10:4–5 · Ephesians 1:19–24 · Ephesians 2:4–6

This is a NOW something — not a later something! This NOW authority is in Yasha’s Name, and it was won by Him through defeating the Kingdom of darkness through His death on the cross and subsequently His resurrection from the dead by the power of Ruach Hakodesh.

Although the rebellion will continue until Yasha returns, all disciples of Christ—Yasha can live in victory over the evil hordes of Satan as they walk in the light and expose the deeds of darkness. Ephesians 5:8

Our authority works through Ruach Hakodesh — not through the flesh. Romans 8:5–7

Our authority works through the Word of AhaYah, including His Torah. His Torah — the first five books of Scripture — provides spiritual understanding. The Old Testament and the New Testament must be read and understood from the culture they were written from.

Our authority works through prayer, and empowers disciples to live in victory.

The Nine-Day Journey

Daily Assignments & Fieldwork

1
FULL INTAKERead the whole teaching · receive the Names
❯

Assignment

Read the entire Session One teaching above, start to finish, in one sitting — aloud where possible. The spoken Word trains both heart and mind.

Fieldwork

  • Write the four Names by hand — AhaYah, Yasha, Ruach haKodesh, Adon — with their meanings.
  • Begin committing Romans 12:2 to memory. You will carry this verse all 66 days.
  • Set your daily appointment time for this course and write it down. Same time, every day.
2
THE NAMESAhaYah · Yasha · Ruach haKodesh · Adon
❯

Assignment

Study the Names section. Yasha is AhaYah — God in the flesh; the Son. Ruach haKodesh is Holy Spirit. Adon is Lord. Your confession that Yasha is Adon and Master over your life is the doorway into deliverance.

Fieldwork

  • Write a personal declaration using each of the four Names, confessing with your own voice that Yasha is Adon and Master over your life.
  • Speak that declaration aloud in prayer.
  • Journal: who has AhaYah been to you until today — and who do you need Him to be through these 66 days?
3
WHAT DELIVERANCE ISBody, soul & spirit cleansing and inner healing
❯

Assignment

Re-read Part A. Then read Isaiah 61:1 and Luke 4:14–21 slowly, twice. Deliverance is for ALL disciples of Yasha — it encompasses body, soul, and spirit cleansing and inner healing, obtained and maintained through a lifestyle of holiness and service unto the Adon.

Fieldwork

  • Write the definition of spiritual deliverance in your own words without looking at the text. Then compare against the teaching and correct.
  • Privately list the areas of your body, soul, and spirit where you know cleansing and inner healing are needed. This list is between you and AhaYah — you will use it again in Session Five.
4
THE KINGDOM OF LIGHTFaith · love · joy · peace · hope
❯

Assignment

Read every Kingdom of Light Scripture: Daniel 7:13–14; Matthew 6:33; Mark 1:14–15; Luke 6:20; Luke 13:18–21; Luke 17:20–21; John 8:1; Colossians 1:13; 1 John 1:5; 1 Peter 2:9. This is the legal realm of your Creator — the realm you should want to live in for eternity.

Fieldwork

  • Beside each passage, write one sentence: what does this reveal about the Kingdom of Light?
  • Make a five-line chart — faith, love, joy, peace, hope — and record where each one is presently evident, or absent, in your life.
5
THE ILLEGAL REALMExposing the adversary’s bondage
❯

Assignment

Read Luke 22:31–34; John 3:19–20; Ephesians 6:12; Colossians 1:13; 1 John 5:19. Satan’s realm is illegal — it has kept human beings in bondage from the beginning, producing suffering, shame, guilt, and condemnation.

Fieldwork

  • Build a two-column contrast chart: the Kingdom of Light’s fruit on one side; the adversary’s produce (suffering, shame, guilt, condemnation) on the other.
  • Journal honestly: where has the adversary presented his realm to you as attractive — and what did embracing it actually cost?
6
AUTHORITY IN HIS NAMEThe Great Commission for all believers
❯

Assignment

Read Psalm 8:4–6; Matthew 18:18; Matthew 28:18–20; Mark 16:15–20; Luke 10:18–24; John 16:33; John 17:26; 2 Corinthians 10:4–5; Ephesians 1:19–24; Ephesians 2:4–6. Your authority and identity are in Yasha—Christ and in His Name alone.

  • From today’s passages, list every statement of what Yasha has been given and what He has given to His disciples.
7
NOW AUTHORITYThrough Ruach, the Word & prayer — not the flesh
❯

Assignment

This is a NOW something — not a later something. Read Romans 8:5–7 and Ephesians 5:8. Your authority works through Ruach Hakodesh, not the flesh; through the Word of AhaYah including His Torah — the first five books, which provide spiritual understanding, read from the culture they were written from; and through prayer.

Fieldwork

  • In prayer, thank Yasha aloud that His authority was won at the cross and the resurrection — and that it is yours NOW, in His Name.
  • Write the three channels your authority works through (Ruach Hakodesh, the Word including Torah, prayer) and one practical way you will engage each channel daily.
