This Module’s Purpose

Why This Session Exists

This session walks the disciple through the Steps to Spiritual Freedom: self-examination, repentance through every level until it is irrevocable, forgiveness, renunciation, the commanding of spirits in Yasha’s Name, and the mandatory filling with Ruach HaKodesh. Bring your Knot Map from Session Four — this is the week the knots are untied.

Review — Bringing What You Have Learned Back to the Table

You carry NOW authority (Session One). You know oppression operates only through legal grounds (Session Two). You have revoked the access your own mouth granted (Session Three). You have seen every strand, traced every knot, found every door, and marked each one for the breaking (Session Four). Now the breaking comes — in order. The steps have a sequence, and the sequence is not optional.

READ THIS BEFORE YOU TAKE ONE MORE STEP — THE FORGIVENESS GATE

Forgiveness is the act of forgiving others and being forgiven — and forgiving others immediately releases you from the tormentors. If you desire to be forgiven, you must be willing to forgive ALL who have harmed you — whether the offense was perceived, imagined, or experienced. No matter the offense, you must be willing to truly forgive.

If you are not willing to forgive, do not continue this course of action — or you could become worse. And hear the words of Yasha Himself, with no softening: “For if ye forgive men their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you: but if ye forgive not men their trespasses, NEITHER WILL YOUR FATHER FORGIVE YOUR TRESPASSES.” — Matthew 6:14–15.

This truth divides every class that has ever heard it, and it will divide this one. Some will stop the work here. Some will be offended and walk away from the faith altogether. And some will do the most dangerous thing of all: double down — insisting they are truly saved and converted while flatly refusing to forgive. To that third road Scripture speaks plainly: the unmerciful servant who received forgiveness of ten thousand talents and would not release his fellow servant’s hundred pence was delivered to the tormentors — “So likewise shall my heavenly Father do also unto you, if ye from your hearts forgive not every one his brother their trespasses” (Matthew 18:21–35). If any man say, I love God, and hateth his brother, he is a liar (1 John 4:20). Unforgiveness is not a personality trait to manage. It is a legal ground the tormentors hold — and no deliverance proceeds over an offense you are still gripping.

There is good news in the hard word: forgiveness is a decision of the will, not a feeling. You do not have to feel it to choose it — and the moment you choose it, the tormentors lose their claim. The gate is narrow, but it opens.

Lesson Five

The Steps to Spiritual Freedom

Step 1 · Self-Examination & Personal Accountability

Self-examination and personal accountability is the first step on the journey to finding freedom in Yasha. This means humbling yourself before your Creator, AhaYah, confessing all known, unknown, and hidden sin with genuine heartfelt repentance. Not just going through the motions; not one trip down the aisle to the altar, crying and getting all emotional; not being sorry you got caught; not being concerned about what other people think — but a true godly repentance which causes one to admit they have offended their Creator and are deeply grieved by it. Godly sorrow leads to repentance. 2 Corinthians 7:10

The turning away from the behaviors, the sins, and the lifestyle you have been living, and turning back to AhaYah after acknowledging that you have sinned against Him and Him only, is the start of a life-long journey — not a quick fix, not a one-time occurrence. Each day is a new opportunity to grow in holiness and continue the deliverance and healing journey. Matthew 3:2–12 · John 16:7–11 · Romans 10:9 · Romans 12:1–3 · Ephesians 4:22–24

Step 2 · Repentance — Through Every Level, Until Irrevocable

Repentance is the next key to opening the path to your freedom in Yasha. These Hebrew and Greek words take you on a journey through repentance that few people ever take. Many believers are unaware there are different levels of repentance — and that we should strive to reach each level until our repentance is irrevocable.

Step 3 · Forgiveness — The Gate That Cannot Be Bypassed

Forgiving others immediately releases you from the tormentors. If you desire to be forgiven, you must be willing to forgive all who have harmed you — perceived, imagined, or experienced. No matter the offense, you must be willing to truly forgive. If you do not forgive, your Father in heaven WILL NOT forgive you. Matthew 6:14–15

Step 4 · Renunciation & Commanding — Then the Mandatory Filling

The next steps to freedom include renouncing evil; breaking all vows, pacts, partnerships, and associations with the enemy; and commanding spirits to leave in Yasha’s Name. Once a thorough renunciation and destruction of all yokes is completed, the filling of these areas with Ruach HaKodesh and AhaYah’s TRUTH is mandatory. If one does not fill these spiritual voids with Ruach HaKodesh, the enemy will return, claim legal rights to the space, and bring more evil spirits with it when it returns to afflict the person again. Matthew 12:43–45

Step 5 · Prayer, Fasting & What to Expect — Manifestations and Ministry Protocols

Prayer, fasting, and a strong foundation in the Word of AhaYah are essential on the road to recovering spiritual freedom. Be prepared to experience resistance to this ministry from these evil spirits, and be prepared for the physical manifestations that can occur during deliverance ministry — coughing, gagging, throwing up, sighing, crying, shaking, convulsions, sexual stimulation, passing gas — as well as the spiritual manifestations which can only be discerned by the leading of Ruach HaKodesh.

During the prayer ministry of deliverance, stay calm with an even tone and a soft voice. Spirits come out in the Name of Yasha, by those with authority — and they are not deaf. Theatrics do not impress demons. Deliverance should be kept private — not done for show on Sunday mornings during fellowship — and administered with safety in mind for those being delivered, protecting others, especially children and new converts who have not yet been delivered themselves.

Deliverance ministers must be taught proper procedure so they and those around them are protected from backlash and retaliation. Under no circumstances should anyone ever agree to allow evil spirits to enter themselves in order to protect someone else — that is tempting AhaYah. The authority in Yasha’s Name, ministered in Yasha’s Name, is effective, thorough, and complete; pride and presumption should not be in the minister’s vocabulary. Ministers should be filled with Ruach haKodesh and prepared through prayer and fasting as led by the Adon prior to all sessions — and after every session, each person involved should pray, seek to be re-filled, and keep the habit of cleansing and protection prayers.

Step 6 · Deliverance Is the Beginning — Not the End Game

Deliverance is not the end game, but the beginning of sustained freedom for each person who seeks to truly follow the ancient path as a disciple of Yasha. Walking in lasting freedom requires crucifying the flesh and aligning with AhaYah’s principles. Inner healing and maintaining peace in Yasha require daily effort, and the Word of AhaYah and fellowship with the brethren must be ongoing parts of each believer’s lifestyle. And one step more: those who have been delivered must learn to share their own story and journey through deliverance, becoming laborers in Yasha’s harvest — for all disciples are called to be reconcilers and deliverers in the Great Commission.

Lesson Summarization — Three Truths to Carry

The steps have a sequence: examination, repentance, forgiveness, renunciation, filling — and the sequence is not optional.  ·  Forgiveness is the gate no deliverance bypasses: forgive, or you will not be forgiven.  ·  Every emptied room must be filled with Ruach HaKodesh, or the enemy returns with reinforcements.
