This Module’s Purpose

Why This Session Exists

This session equips disciples of Yasha to recognize, identify, and close legal doors that allow demonic oppression to operate in the body, soul, and spirit. Freedom is sustained not by ignorance, but by truth, discernment, repentance, and authority.

Foundational Understanding

Demonic influence does not occur randomly. It always operates through legal grounds — agreements, permissions, wounds, sins, vows, and deceptions that grant access.

“My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge. And because you have rejected My Torah, I have rejected you from being kings and priests, and I will forget your children.” — Hosea 4:6
Lesson Two

The Nature of Demonic Oppression

A · Legal Grounds & Open Doors

Understand how demonic influence and oppression can occur in a disciple’s life through legal grounds and open doors in our body, soul, and spirit. Discover patterns of unresolved mental and emotional issues that stem from division, unusual fears, intrusive thoughts, hopelessness, compulsion toward sin, unconfessed sin, past traumas, and past soul ties that have not been broken — including open doors such as occult involvement, unforgiveness, idolatry, and many others.

Matthew 16:23 — Corruption of thoughts  ·  2 Corinthians 12:7 — Mental & physical torment  ·  Acts 5:3 — Heart & speech  ·  1 Thessalonians 2:18 — Thwarting ministry efforts

B · Strongholds: Common Signs & Manifestations

Soul — mental and emotional oppression and depression: intense irrational fear, chronic hopelessness, severe doubts, intrusive and negative thoughts, constant accusations of self and others, extreme restlessness, inability to focus, strong pulling toward forbidden things and/or the occult.

Body — behavioral mannerisms: compulsion to sin, iniquitous and generational sin, stirring up conflict, gossip, causing division, increased hostility toward God or deliverance ministers, withdrawing from others.

Spirit — spiritual oppression and torment: procrastination, feeling oppressed and depressed, extreme heaviness, hindered in serving God, a sense of powerlessness, an overall feeling of fatigue and tiredness, physical limitations that cannot be medically diagnosed.

C · The Who and Why of It

The adversary — Satan, the accuser of the brethren — and his demons seek to destroy, tempt, or hinder humans’ effectiveness on earth, seeking to sift disciples as wheat according to Luke 22:31–32. The open doors previously discussed form an extensive list, examined spirit by spirit in Session Four.

D · Methodology the Enemy Uses

Mind binding, influencing thoughts, projecting thoughts into you causing you to think it was your own thought, tempting one to commit sin, using fear tactics, mind control, and hindering spiritual growth — all tactics used to hinder one’s spiritual growth. 1 Timothy 4:1

E · Oppression vs. Possession — And Your Assurance

Oppression is ongoing influence and attack, while possession is a more complete control — both can be addressed and overcome through Christ. Romans 8:37

When Father became flesh and walked the earth as Yasha the Son, He made it clear that demonic oppression and possession would be a thing of the past — for the Jew first and then the Gentile. Deliverance and healing is for all who have accepted Him as Savior and made Him Adon and Master over themselves.

It is Scripturally correct to teach that believers cannot be possessed, and that non-believers (unregenerated spirits) can be possessed by these hordes. 1 John 4:4 · 1 Corinthians 3:16 · Ephesians 1:13–14

Hear This Reassurance

Many of the behaviors and manifestations you will inventory this week were triggered — defense mechanisms you unknowingly practiced or exhibited, because no one ever taught you the WHO and the WHY behind them. Oppression is the problem; the flesh reacts. But as a true believer you cannot be possessed. You are the temple of AhaYah, sealed by Ruach HaKodesh — and greater is He that is in you than he that is in the world.

Lesson Summarization — Three Truths to Carry

Demonic oppression always requires legal access.  ·  Strongholds are always identifiable — and they are always removable.  ·  Your victory is enforced through truth, repentance, and Yasha’s authority.

The Five Questions
