This Module’s Purpose

Why This Session Exists

This session opens the participant’s eyes to the creative and destructive power of the tongue. Death and life are in the power of the tongue, and they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof (Proverbs 18:21). Here the disciple learns to recognize curses — generational, spoken, self-imposed, occult, and sin-linked — to discern the hidden symbolism and everyday snares buried in modern language, and to take the helm of their own ship by setting a watch over their mouth.

Review — Bringing What You Have Learned Back to the Table

In Session One you learned that your authority is a NOW authority, working through Ruach Hakodesh, the Word, and prayer — and that authority is exercised by what you speak in Yasha’s Name. In Session Two you learned that demonic influence is never random: it always operates through legal grounds — agreements, permissions, wounds, sins, vows, and deceptions. Now join the two truths and see what they reveal together: your mouth is the most frequently used door in the house. Every word is either an exercise of your authority or a granting of legal access. Your own thoughts — including the projected thoughts you learned to test in Session Two — become your conversations, and your conversations shape your world. Out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaketh (Matthew 12:34).

Lesson Three

Curses & the Power of Words

A · The Five Families of Curses

Generational curses — iniquity visiting down family lines. Exodus 20:5 · Numbers 14:18 · Lamentations 5:7

Spoken / word curses — words spoken over you in general conversation, and vows and inner vows spoken over you by others, such as authority figures in your life. These can be used for spiritual manipulation and cause spiritual stagnation.

Self-imposed curses — words you speak over yourself, including rash vows and oaths. Jacob unknowingly spoke over Rachel — “with whomsoever thou findest thy gods, let him not live” — and Rachel died on the journey. Genesis 31:32 & 35:19 · Leviticus 20:9 · Proverbs 18:21 · Matthew 12:37 · Matthew 27:25 · Acts 23:14

Witchcraft / occult curses — curses arising from forbidden practices. Deuteronomy 18:10–12 · Galatians 5:19–21

Curses linked to sin or broken vows — the whole catalogue of Deuteronomy 28 stands as witness, and Scripture warns: better not to vow, than to vow and not pay. Deuteronomy 28 · Ecclesiastes 5:4–6

“For by thy words thou shalt be justified, and by thy words thou shalt be condemned.” — Matthew 12:37

B · Death and Life Are in the Power of the Tongue — and the Fruits Thereof

Proverbs 18:21 does not say the tongue describes life and death — it says death and life are in its power. And they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof: your mouth plants orchards, and you will eat from whichever orchard you planted.

The fruits of death-speech — condemnation by your own words (Matthew 12:37); being snared and taken by the words of your mouth (Proverbs 6:2); piercings of a sword in relationships (Proverbs 12:18); spiritual stagnation under inner vows; destruction of the home and reputation; a fire that defiles the whole body and sets the course of nature ablaze (James 3:6); and heaven’s record of every idle word (Matthew 12:36). AhaYah told Israel plainly: “As ye have spoken in mine ears, so will I do to you” — Numbers 14:28. They said “we shall die in this wilderness,” and they did.

The fruits of life-speech — a wholesome tongue is a tree of life (Proverbs 15:4); pleasant words are health to the bones (Proverbs 16:24); the mouth that keeps itself keeps its soul from troubles (Proverbs 21:23); the one who would love life and see good days refrains the tongue from evil (1 Peter 3:10); grace ministered to every hearer (Ephesians 4:29); mountains moved by the believer who says and doubts not (Mark 11:23); and the decreed thing established (Job 22:28).

C · Hidden Symbolism & the Snares in Everyday Language

Thou art snared with the words of thy mouth (Proverbs 6:2). Modern language is full of snares the believer repeats without ever being taught what stands behind them:

“Luck” and “fortune” — Isaiah 65:11 rebukes those who prepare a table for Gad (the god of fortune, “luck”) and furnish the drink offering unto Meni (the god of destiny). Every “good luck,” “lucky charm,” and “fortune favors me” tips the hat to idols Scripture names.

Occult-rooted habits of speech and gesture — “knock on wood” (appealing to spirits in trees), “cross your fingers,” horoscope and zodiac talk (“that’s such a Gemini thing”), “the universe will provide,” “karma will get them” — each one borrows the vocabulary of divination and false religion examined in Deuteronomy 18:10–12.

Casual death-speech — “I’m dying to see it,” “that’s to die for,” “I’m sick to death of this,” “scared to death,” “this job is killing me,” “I could just die.” The mouth invites what the heart never intended.

Self-curses dressed as humility or humor — “I’m so stupid,” “I never get anything right,” “nothing good ever happens to me,” “I’ll always be broke,” “sickness runs in my family so I’ll get it too.” These are agreements — legal grounds granted by your own testimony against yourself.

Oaths and idle vows — “I swear,” “cross my heart and hope to die,” “on my mother’s grave.” Yasha said: let your communication be Yea, yea; Nay, nay — for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil. Matthew 5:37

D · The Tiny Member That Steers the Ship

Behold also the ships: though they are so great and driven of fierce winds, yet they are turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor listeth. Even so the tongue is a little member — and it boasts great things. The same tiny rudder that wrecks a vessel on the rocks can steer it through the waters of life into harbor. No man can tame the tongue — but Ruach HaKodesh can, when the disciple sets the watch: “Set a watch, O AhaYah, before my mouth; keep the door of my lips.” James 3:2–12 · Psalm 141:3

Lesson Summarization — Three Truths to Carry

Words are never neutral — every word exercises authority or grants access.  ·  The curse causeless cannot land — and its legal grounds can always be removed.  ·  The tongue is the helm: whoever governs it steers the whole ship.
