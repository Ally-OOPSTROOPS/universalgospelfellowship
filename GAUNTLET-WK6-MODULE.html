Lesson Six

Inner Healing, Broken Hearts, Soul Ties & Lies

A · The Anointing and the Blueprint

“The Spirit of the Adon, the AhlaHayam of Israel, is upon me; because the Adon has anointed me to preach good tidings unto the meek; he has sent me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives, and the opening of the prison to them that are bound.” — Isaiah 61:1

Yasha Himself quoted Isaiah 61:1 when He stood in the synagogue, having returned from the Jordan and the wilderness, led by Ruach Hakodesh: “He has anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he has sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised, to preach the acceptable year of the Adon.” Then He closed the book, gave it to the minister, and sat down — and all eyes were upon Him (Luke 4:18).

This is the Scriptural foundation for inner healing and true wholeness — body, soul, and spirit: “Now may the God of peace himself cause you to be completely dedicated to him; and may your spirit, soul, and body be kept intact and blameless at our Adon Yasha’s coming” (1 Thessalonians 5:23). Yasha gave us the blueprint for deliverance ministry: preach the gospel, heal the brokenhearted, preach deliverance to those held captive at Hasatan’s will, and restore sight to the blind — spiritual blindness more than physical — setting at liberty all that are bruised.

B · Access to the Promises

Once the gospel has been presented to one that is meek, they decide to accept it or reject it. “The sacrifices of AhaYah are a broken spirit; a broken and contrite heart, O AhaYah, you will not despise” (Psalm 51:17) — He wants humility and godly sorrow; when people humble themselves and turn from their sins, He will not refuse them.

After believing the gospel and receiving Yasha as Savior, they have access to inner healing — liberation from captivity, known or unknown spiritual bondage — released from being held captive at the enemy’s will: “They have been trapped by the devil and he makes them obey him, but God may help them escape” (2 Timothy 2:26). A wholly converted human has access to ALL the promises in Scripture. Physical and spiritual cleansing and healing are benefits of becoming a Son of the Most High, the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, the God of Israel, our Creator and Sovereign King: “For no matter how many promises AhaYah has made, they are Yes in Yasha” (2 Corinthians 1:20).

C · Deliverance First — Then the Mending of the Broken Heart

All of us have bruises that need healing and deliverance. Once we go through deliverance, then and only then can inner healing be ministered. Connecting inner healing — based on traumas, fears, abuse, neglect, abandonment, evil intentions — with deliverance is crucial to help someone understand how to sever ungodly soul ties and break unhealthy soul-binding relationships. As revelation comes, they can replace lies with truth.

Once we have experienced deliverance, our inner healing process starts, and we can truly have our minds transformed: “Be not conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind” (Romans 12:2). Continuing prayer ministry reveals through deliverance what areas of the soul need inner healing. Broken hearts can be mended when surrendered completely to the Adon. Effective prayer and continued counsel in the Word are the main tools for those seeking inner healing.

D · Soul Ties, Soul-Binding Relationships & Codependency

The breaking of all ungodly soul ties and soul-binding relationships should be further explored for those who have been in abusive and manipulative relationships. Codependency must be broken as well, so that healthy relationships can be formed in the future. Fragmented souls may take longer to heal and should be handled with great care and consideration — especially children and the emotionally immature, who need further instruction on how to effectively recover from these traumas.

E · Replacing Lies with the Truth — True Spiritual Warfare

This is where true spiritual warfare is experienced. By recognizing the lies, we take steps to change our beliefs to Scriptural truths — defeating the enemy by identifying the recurring, derogatory thoughts and conversations and replacing them with truthful thoughts and statements. If the thought or speech does not align with Scripture, it is from the enemy — and must be spoken to with authority and renounced. Replace negative thoughts, perspectives, and hype with positive thoughts and memorized Scripture, retraining your brain and heart. This process takes discipline and time, but it will eventually take root, and your thoughts and speech will improve.

“If you continue in my word, then you are my disciples indeed; and you will know the truth, and the truth will make you free.” — John 8:31–32. Converts are to be taught how to exchange lies they have believed their entire lives for Scriptural truth.

The weapons of this warfare: Ephesians 6:10–19 (the whole armor of AhaYah, the sword of the Spirit which is the spoken Word) · 2 Corinthians 10:3–6 (casting down imaginations, bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Yasha) · Matthew 12:34–37 (out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks) · 2 Thessalonians 2:8–14 (they perished because they received not the love of the truth — and were sent strong delusion to believe a lie).
